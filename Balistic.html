Strategic Hypersonic Kinematics

I. Comparison of Launch & Trajectory Dynamics

Standard Ballistic Missiles follow a Keplerian Ellipse. Once the boost phase ends, the trajectory is governed solely by gravity and momentum ($ \vec{g} $). In contrast, Hypersonic Glide Vehicles (HGV) decouple from the ballistic arc at the exoatmospheric boundary, utilizing Atmospheric Compression Shocks to glide.

Unlike normal missiles that exit and re-enter, Hypersonic systems skip along the Karman Line, making their interception point mathematically non-deterministic.
